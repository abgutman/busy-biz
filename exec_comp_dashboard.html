How to read this table — and where each number comes from

    A dash (—) means the company's table does not report that column for this officer/year — not a zero. Click details on any row for the verbatim source line, the All Other Compensation footnote, and an arithmetic check of the reported total.

    Total pay over five fiscal years

    The figure is each officer's Total compensation (the Summary Compensation Table's bottom-line column, Item 402(c)(2)(x)) for each of the five most recent fiscal years the company has reported — no category breakdown. Because these companies close their books on different fiscal calendars, columns are aligned by recency, not calendar year: Latest FY is each company's most recently reported year, −1 yr the year before, and so on. Every cell shows the company's actual fiscal year and links to that year's proxy.
    A dash (—) means the officer was not a named executive officer in that year's table — not zero pay. The five-year total sums only the years an officer was named, so officers present for fewer years are not directly comparable on that column. The company total sums each year's named-officer pay as reported; the roster changes year to year.

    Source: U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, EDGAR. Compensation figures transcribed from the Summary Compensation Table (Item 402(c) of Regulation S-K, 17 CFR 229.402) in each company's most recent Form DEF 14A. Filing census from the EDGAR submissions API (data.sec.gov), accessed June 14, 2026. Each row links to the exact filing.

    Scope & method: Company universe is the 114 Philadelphia-region public companies tracked by this newsroom's earnings monitor. "Filed 2026" means a definitive proxy (DEF 14A) with a 2026 filing date; companies with non-December fiscal years (e.g., Aramark, Campbell's) file later in their own cycle and may show a 2025 proxy as their latest. Figures reflect the most recent fiscal year in each table, for the named executive officers only (typically the CEO, CFO and three other highest-paid officers). Compensation was extracted by automated reading of each filing and is pending line-by-line human verification.

    Five-year view: The 5-Year Total Pay tab draws each officer's reported Total compensation from the five most recent annual proxies per company (one fiscal year per filing, taken as reported at the time so departed officers keep their then-current figures). Companies on different fiscal calendars are aligned by recency rather than calendar year — the actual fiscal year is labeled on every cell and each links to its source proxy. Year ranges: most companies FY2021–FY2025; Urban Outfitters FY2022–FY2026; QVC Group FY2020–FY2024.

    Known gaps: QVC Group's latest proxy is from 2025 (the company is in bankruptcy). Foreign private issuers (e.g., AstraZeneca) report on Form 20-F and do not file a DEF 14A. One figure carries a $3 internal rounding discrepancy in Five Below's own filing (flagged on the row). Always confirm against the linked document before publication.
